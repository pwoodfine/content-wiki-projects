Woodfine Management Corp. operates a proprietary retail co-location intelligence
platform designed to identify, rank, and validate commercial real estate nodes
across North America and Europe. This encyclopedia serves as the definitive
reference for the firm's spatial methodologies, ranking algorithms, and regional
deployment indices. All content is structured for institutional review and
adheres to verifiable, data-driven spatial analysis.

**[[co-location-ranking-system|The Retail Co-location Ranking System]]**

The Woodfine co-location ranking system evaluates retail sites using a
deterministic algorithm that scores each hypermarket anchor location based on the
convergence of secondary and tertiary retail and civic categories within defined
catchment radii.

The system produces a 12-rank index mapped to five quality tiers, visualised on
the GIS platform using a warm-to-cool colour scale. At the apex is the **Tier 5
(Full Co-location)**, representing sites where an anchor operates within 3 km of
both a hardware superstore and a warehouse club, and within 5 km of both a
healthcare facility and a university. This indicates that all four independent
capital-selection processes have converged on the exact same geographic node.

## Did you know...

<!-- ENGINE NOTE: the statistics below are derived from the GIS platform
     dataset, not from wiki article metadata. They do not update
     automatically — refresh these figures when the GIS dataset is
     updated. -->

- **Global scale:** The current dataset identifies **2,738 ranked co-location
  sites** globally, encompassing 2,488 in North America and 250 in Europe.
- **Apex scarcity:** As of May 2026, there are **102 Tier 5 (Full Co-location)**
  sites across North America. Rank-1 sites represent 3.7% of total anchors,
  enforcing strict index scarcity.
- **Data provenance:** The platform analyses over 31,000 individual retail
  locations across 60+ chains, fusing OpenStreetMap service-business data with
  Overture Maps Foundation civic infrastructure records.
